Mulligan Strategy for Aggro Paladin
The goal of your mulligan is to establish an early board presence and curve out effectively. You want to prioritize low-cost minions that can develop pressure and survive removal.

General Mulligan Guidelines:
Keep: Murmy, Righteous Protector, Cult Neophyte, Gold Panner, Grimestreet Outfitter, Mining Casualties. These are your core early game minions.
Consider keeping: Muster for Battle if you have other low-cost minions to follow up.
Mulligan: High-cost cards like Sea Giant, Prismatic Beam, and Leeroy Jenkins unless you have a strong early game.
Specific Mulligan Adjustments Based on Opponent:
Against Aggro Decks: Prioritize early game minions and removal like Muster for Battle. Aim to flood the board quickly and establish control.
Against Control Decks: Keep a balance of early game minions and potential threats like Gorgonzormu or Showdown!. You want to pressure them early while also having options for later in the game.
Additional Tips:
Coin: If you win the coin, you can be more aggressive in your mulligan, keeping slightly weaker early game cards.
Adjustments: Mulligan strategy can vary depending on the meta and your personal preferences. Experiment with different approaches to find what works best for you.
